Discover

How to design KKday App? What's different behavior and need of users between desktop and App?

Before designing KKday App, we want to know the different behavior and need of users between desktop and App, so we interviewed 12 KKday users from 18~45 years old, who also be an active e-commerce users. Because half of KKday users come from overseas, in order to distinguish the cultural differences of users, we conducted interviews according to users' language ratio, ensuring the reference value of the research. (Oversea users: by video interview) The followings are feedbacks from users:

 

I like to browse shopping app when I take the MRT, it's nice to kill commuting time. (26 female white-collar worker, Taiwan)

I usually use desktop if I order a product, because it is more convenient to fill in the order information. (30 housewife, Taiwan)

When I use KKday, I want to discover interesting and special itineraries. (28 male white-collar worker, Japan)

I will add passenger tickets, hotel tickets and attraction tickets into Apple wallet when I am traveling, so I won't be scramble and mess something up. (32 male photographer, HK)

Actually, I don't focus too much on product detail when I order a trip. Price, date and location are more important to me. (35 male writer, US)

I prefer to add products into shopping cart in app first, then carefully browse product detail on desktop. (25 female white-collar worker, Korean)

Key Finding from Interviews:

1. When browsing e-commerce App, users are more likely to add products into wishlist or shopping cart.
2. When browsing e-commerce App, users will more focus on photos rather than detail information
3. When browsing KKday on desktop, users want to compare each products, so they will open each product pages, finding best price–performance ratio one.
4. When ordering KKday products, users need to fill in a lot personal information, which is troublesome by using App. We have to figure out an efficient and easy App ordering process.
5. When traveling, users would like to save all tickets and vouchers in Apple wallet. It can reduce the troubles or risks during the trip.

Visual Design

As an UX design intern, I mainly focus on design of voucher, orderlist, login and filter pages in KKday App.

The following is KKday style guideline:

- Login Page:

Users can login by Google accounts, Facebook, or other social login based on user's country. We support Naver and Kakao login for Korean, Line and Yahoo login for Japan, Wechat and QQ login for China and HK according to questionnaires and usability tests.

- Order List Page:

Users can check orders by clicking tab bar, it will show the trip that is coming soon/already had/cancled. The main information that users care about in this page are order numbers, dates, locations, contact CS and voucher channel.

- Voucher:

Since creating a smooth, snag-free travel experience is always our goal, the feature of adding vouchers into Apple wallet is important as we got feedbacks from interviews before. It can reduce the risk of forgeting any ticket or being stolen. Also, users can check detail information of each itinerary or location before heading out, reduce the feeling of nervous or anxiety.

- Filter page:

Users can filter or sort products by clicking the button displayed above the tap bar. The products can sort by popularity, reviews or price, it can also filter by product categories, dates, languages, trip duration or budget, let users quickly find the amazing trip they need.

Usability Testing

Before KKday app officially released, we led several usability tests accroding to different features and scenerios. We tested our wireframes against key tasks with Invion prototype and beta versions. This helped us identify user frustrations, so we could improve our designs and provide a smooth travel experience.

 

 

We also invited cross-functional teams, such as project owner, front-end developer, back-end developer to observe uability testing. It's helpful to reach an agreement, also come up with a wider variety of ideas.

Recordings

One way to meausure the design of products is to recorded heatmap and video every two weeks following with development sprint. Heatmaps aggregate user interactions, include touch, tap, swipe, pinch and scroll in KKday. We also record videos for user flow to understand and explain "why" users do instead of "what", it is helpful to know users' real needs behind the bahaviors and keep optimizing our products.
